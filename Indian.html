Classic Indian Recipes

Chana Masala

North Indian-style chickpea curry made with onions, tomatoes, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add tomatoes and cook until they're soft.
  3. Add chickpeas, cumin powder, coriander powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Cook for 10-15 minutes or until the flavors have melded together.

Palak Paneer

Spinach curry made with paneer (Indian cheese), spinach puree, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams paneer
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch spinach leaves in boiling water and puree them in a blender.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions and garlic until golden brown.
  3. Add paneer and cook until it's lightly browned.
  4. Add spinach puree, cumin powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  5. Cook for 5-7 minutes or until the flavors have melded together.

Samosa

Crispy fried or baked pastries filled with spiced potatoes and peas.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • Filling ingredients: boiled potatoes, peas, onions, cumin seeds, coriander powder, garam masala powder, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Mix together flour, salt, and baking powder.
  2. Add ghee or oil and mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions.
  5. Roll out each portion into a thin circle.
  6. Place a tablespoon or two of the filling in the center of each circle.
  7. Fold the dough over the filling to form a triangle or cone shape.
  8. Seal the edges with a little water.
  9. Fry or bake the samosas until crispy and golden brown.

Pani Puri

Ingredients:- For Puri: - 2 cups all-purpose flour - 1/2 teaspoon salt - 1/4 teaspoon baking powder - 1/4 cup ghee or oil - 1/2 cup lukewarm water For Pani: - 1 cup tamarind water - 1 cup mint chutney - 1/2 cup green chutney - 1/2 cup lemon juice - Salt, to taste - Ice cubes For Filling: - Boiled and mashed chickpeas - Boiled and diced potatoes - Chopped onions, tomatoes, and cilantro

Instructions:

  • ix flour, salt, and baking powder.
  • Add ghee/oil and mix.
  • Gradually add water and knead.
  • Shape into small balls and flatten.
  • Fry until golden brown.
  • Mix pani ingredients and chill.
  • Assemble puri by filling with chickpeas, potatoes, onions, and pani.

Pav Bhaji

Ingredients: - 1 cup mixed vegetables (potatoes, carrots, peas, cauliflower) - 1 tablespoon butter - 1 onion, chopped - 2 cloves garlic, minced - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon Pav Bhaji masala - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - Salt, to taste - 4-6 pav buns - Lemon wedges, chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions:

  • Boil mixed vegetables until tender.
  • Heat butter in a pan, add onion, garlic, and cumin seeds.
  • Add Pav Bhaji masala, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Mash boiled vegetables and add to the pan. Mix well.
  • Toast pav buns and serve with bhaji.
  • Garnish with lemon wedges and chopped cilantro (if using).

Aloo Matar

Ingredients: - 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced - 1 cup fresh or frozen peas - 2 medium onions, chopped - 2 cloves garlic, minced - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon garam masala powder - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - Salt, to taste - 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
  • Add onions and garlic, sauté until onions are translucent.
  • Add potatoes, peas, garam masala powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Cook until potatoes are tender and peas are cooked.
  • Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
